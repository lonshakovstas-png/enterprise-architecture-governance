## Definition

A decision is architecturally significant if it satisfies one or more of the following criteria:

(a) It affects the structure, principles, or boundaries of the corporate information system.

(b) It creates, modifies, or removes a governance constraint applicable to the corporate information system.

(c) It affects multiple system domains or crosses organisational boundaries.

(d) It would be difficult or impossible to reverse without significant cost, disruption, or risk.
