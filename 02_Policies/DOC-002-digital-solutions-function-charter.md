## Purpose

This charter establishes the mandate, scope of responsibility, and authority of the Digital Solutions function in relation to the governance of corporate architecture and the corporate information system.

It defines the function's position within the Enterprise Architecture Governance Framework and its authority to act in accordance with the governance baseline established by ADR-0011 and DOC-001.

## Scope

This charter applies to the Digital Solutions function and defines its governance mandate over all initiatives, projects, decisions and changes affecting the corporate information system.

## Provisions

### Mandate

The Digital Solutions function is the owner and custodian of the Enterprise Architecture Governance Framework.

The function is empowered to:

* Define and maintain architecture governance policies, procedures, and standards within the framework
* Maintain the enterprise-architecture-governance repository as the primary authoritative governance baseline
* Ensure that architecturally significant decisions are formally recorded as ADRs prior to implementation
* Define the canonical document types, authority hierarchy, and compliance requirements that govern architecture artifacts within scope

### Authority

The Digital Solutions function is responsible for ensuring that architecturally significant decisions are governed in accordance with the Enterprise Architecture Governance Framework.

The function is accountable for:

* The structure, content, and integrity of the Enterprise Architecture Governance Framework
* The acceptance or revision of governance artifacts prior to their approval and inclusion in the governance baseline

The Digital Solutions function does not hold authority over:

* Operational or project delivery decisions below the threshold of architectural significance
* Organisational structure, resourcing, or operations of other functions

### Relationship with the Enterprise Architecture Governance Framework

The Digital Solutions function is responsible for maintaining and administering the Enterprise Architecture Governance Framework. Its mandate is established by ADR-0011 and governed by DOC-001.

The function shall ensure that:

* All governance artifacts within scope conform to the canonical document types defined by the framework
* The document authority hierarchy — ADR > DOC > REG > STD — is upheld across all governance artifacts
* Changes to the framework structure are introduced through a new or superseding ADR in accordance with P-05 of DOC-001

The function shall not act in a manner that conflicts with any approved ADR or with the provisions of DOC-001.

## Responsibilities

**Head of Information Systems Development**

The Head of Information Systems Development holds executive accountability for the Digital Solutions function and the governance baseline it maintains.

The Head of Information Systems Development formally approves:

* New governance artifacts
* Major revisions
* Supersessions
* Withdrawals

in accordance with REG-001.

**Digital Solutions Function**

The Digital Solutions Function is collectively responsible for:

* Owning, maintaining, and evolving the Enterprise Architecture Governance Framework
* Ensuring the enterprise-architecture-governance repository reflects the current approved governance baseline at all times
* Reviewing all change proposals submitted under REG-001
* Approving Minor Amendments in accordance with REG-001
* Escalating governance decisions where required

**All Parties Within Scope**

All teams, projects, and initiatives within scope are responsible for operating in conformance with the Digital Solutions Function's governance mandate as defined in this charter and in DOC-001.

## Review

This charter shall be reviewed semi-annually from its approval date, or upon any change to the Enterprise Architecture Governance Framework structure or to the mandate of the Digital Solutions Function, whichever occurs first.
